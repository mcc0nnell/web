    04

    The access transaction

    From institutional output to completed protective action.

    Burden is measured per transaction, not per census tract. It can accumulate at any stage between an official message being issued and a person completing the instructed action—an adaptation of the logic of the Protective Action Decision Model.

    Institutional outputCompleted protective action

    1. 1

      Issued

      Official information is released to the public.

    2. 2

      Discovered

      The person finds out the information exists.

    3. 3

      Represented

      They obtain an accessible representation of it.

    4. 4

      Understood

      They understand what it means.

    5. 5

      Verified

      They confirm accuracy, relevance, and currency.

    6. 6

      Acted

      They identify and complete the required action.

    Compensatory labor can begin hereand compound across the transaction

    A system can satisfy issuance and still fail the person at any later stage—which is precisely what the index is built to detect.

    05

    The gap and the instrument

    The research gap

    Everyone measures the fragments. No one has combined them.

    The literature consistently documents individual barriers, access failures, and institutional deficits. What is missing is a validated composite measure that would:

    • Quantify compensatory labor imposed on the individual
    • Link separate burdens into latent dimensions
    • Tie burden to protective-action outcomes
    • Attribute burden to institutional or technical sources
    • Run before and after an intervention
    • Serve as research instrument and performance metric
    Operational value

    From academic construct to public contract.

    The index gives a Deaf-led access layer a measurable proposition: measure the ordinary pathway, insert verified information and accessible production upstream, then measure again and report the reduction.

    • Median time to usable information
    • Number of channels consulted
    • Caption and interpretation repair steps
    • Dependence on informal networks
    • Unanswered accommodation requests
    • Failed or delayed protective actions
    Concluding proposition

    The quality of emergency access is inversely related to the amount of compensatory labor disabled people must perform to make the system work.
